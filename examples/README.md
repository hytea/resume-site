# Configuration Examples

This folder contains example configuration files to help you get started with your resume website. Each example demonstrates different use cases and customization approaches.

## Available Examples

### 1. Junior Developer (`config-junior-developer.json`)

**Best for:** Entry-level developers, coding bootcamp graduates, career changers

**Features:**
- Emphasis on recent education and training
- Focus on eagerness to learn and grow
- Smaller project portfolio
- Fresh, modern color scheme (blue/purple)
- Highlights relevant internships and junior positions

**Use this if:**
- You have 0-3 years of professional experience
- You recently completed a bootcamp or degree
- You want to showcase learning projects

---

### 2. Designer/Creative (`config-designer.json`)

**Best for:** UX/UI designers, graphic designers, creative directors

**Features:**
- Visual-first portfolio presentation
- Emphasis on design process and impact metrics
- Creative color scheme (pink/purple/amber)
- Links to Behance, Figma, design portfolios
- Design-centric language and terminology

**Use this if:**
- You work in design or creative fields
- Your portfolio is a key part of your resume
- You want to showcase design thinking and user research

---

### 3. Minimal (`config-minimal.json`)

**Best for:** Anyone who prefers simplicity and clarity

**Features:**
- Clean, straightforward layout
- Simple color scheme (blue/green)
- Concise descriptions
- Focus on essential information only
- Less visual flourish, more content focus

**Use this if:**
- You prefer a clean, no-nonsense approach
- You're in a traditional industry
- You want to focus on content over style
- You're just getting started and want something simple

---

## How to Use These Examples

### Method 1: Direct Copy

1. Choose an example that fits your profile
2. Copy the entire content of the example file
3. Replace your `public/config.json` with this content
4. Customize the values with your own information

### Method 2: Use as Reference

1. Open an example file alongside your `config.json`
2. Copy specific sections you like
3. Adapt the language and structure to fit your needs
4. Mix and match from multiple examples

### Method 3: Import into Config Editor

1. Open `config-editor.html` in your browser
2. Click "Import config.json"
3. Select one of these example files
4. The form will populate with the example data
5. Edit the values to match your information
6. Download your customized config

## Customization Tips

### Personalizing Your Example

After choosing an example, customize these key areas:

1. **Personal Information**
   - Update all names, contact info, and locations
   - Change years of experience
   - Update website and social links

2. **Colors**
   - Each example uses different colors
   - Modify `theme.primaryColor`, `theme.secondaryColor`, `theme.accentColor`
   - Use a [color picker](https://htmlcolorcodes.com/color-picker/) to find your perfect palette

3. **Content Tone**
   - Junior example: enthusiastic, eager to learn
   - Designer example: creative, impact-focused
   - Minimal example: straightforward, professional
   - Adjust the tone to match your personality

4. **Portfolio Items**
   - Replace example projects with your own work
   - Use real URLs and screenshots
   - Write descriptions that highlight your contributions

5. **Experience**
   - Replace with your actual work history
   - Match the description style to your example
   - Include relevant metrics and achievements

## Mixing and Matching

You can combine elements from different examples:

**Example:** "I want the Junior Developer's enthusiastic tone, the Designer's visual focus, and the Minimal example's clean structure"

1. Start with `config-minimal.json` as your base
2. Copy the `portfolio` section from `config-designer.json`
3. Adapt the language style from `config-junior-developer.json`
4. Create your own unique blend!

## Creating Your Own Style

Once you're comfortable with these examples, create your own unique configuration:

1. **Choose a Color Scheme**
   - Pick colors that represent your personality or brand
   - Use tools like [Coolors](https://coolors.co/) for color palette inspiration

2. **Write Authentic Content**
   - Be genuine in your bio and descriptions
   - Highlight what makes you unique
   - Use specific examples and metrics

3. **Select Your Best Work**
   - Quality over quantity in your portfolio
   - Show diverse skills and projects
   - Include projects you're proud of

4. **Test and Iterate**
   - Preview your site locally
   - Ask friends for feedback
   - Refine and improve over time
